Glasgow: The City That Doesn’t Try to Please Everyone

Glasgow is Scotland’s largest city and arguably the most underrated in the UK. If Edinburgh is the polished official portrait in a kilt with bagpipes, Glasgow is its younger brother who showed up to the family photo in a leather jacket, with a tattoo and a sly grin. While the capital poses elegantly for the tourists, Glasgow is already halfway down the pub with your wallet, buying everyone a round and telling better stories...

Quick facts:

  • Population: ~630,000 within the city proper, around 1.8 million in the greater area
  • Founded: 12th century, but its real boom came in the 19th century — Victorian era and the “second city of the British Empire”
  • Nicknames: “Dear Green Place” (thanks to over 90 parks), and locally just Glesga or Weegie
  • Subway: Home to the oldest subway in the world after London’s (the famous orange “Clockwork Orange”, opened in 1896)
  • Museum: Has more than 20 museums and galleries with free entry, including the Kelvingrove and the Riverside Museum of Transport

Top 5 must to do in Glasgow

  1. Have a pint in one of the old pubs in the Finnieston area.
  2. Stroll through the Necropolis, a Victorian cemetery with incredible views of the city.
  3. Go to a concert at the Barrowland Ballroom
  4. Try a deep-fried Mars bar (yes, it exists, and yes, it's delicious)
  5. Get lost in the West End and accidentally find the best coffee and street art of your life.
